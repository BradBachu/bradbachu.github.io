Welcome

I'm a Research Scientist at Uniswap Labs, where I work on the Protocols Research team. My research focuses on decentralized trading systems — including auction design, AMMs, liquidity provision, and execution quality.

Before joining Uniswap, I completed a PhD in theoretical physics at Princeton University. My dissertation explored scattering amplitudes in quantum field theory using on-shell techniques. I continue to bring this mindset — precise modeling and rigorous analysis — to the evolving landscape of crypto markets.

I grew up in Trinidad and Tobago, earned a double major in Physics and Mathematics at MIT, and remain passionate about applying theoretical ideas to practical systems. My work today spans data analysis, mechanism design, simulations, and protocol optimization.

See research for past and current work, or other for interests outside of crypto and physics.
